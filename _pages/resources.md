This page is a compilation of resources I find helpful in exploring the relationship between neurons and cognition:

## Allen Brain Institute
- **Allen Brain Cell Atlas:** [https://brain-map.org/bkp/explore/abc-atlas](https://brain-map.org/bkp/explore/abc-atlas)
	- This is a great resource for examining the transcriptomics and spatial sequencing (MERFISH) data in humans and mice.
	- For example, CHRNA2->α2nAChRs and is expressed quite exclusively in L5 Martinotti Cells of the mouse. However, looking at human scRNA data, we see it is expressed throughout VIP interneurons but not SST, hinting at different effects of nicotine on human cognition compared to the mouse. This is relevant for understanding translational applications of nicotinic treatment for, e.g., auditory processing disorder.
- **MICrONS Explorer:** [https://www.microns-explorer.org/cortical-mm3](https://www.microns-explorer.org/cortical-mm3)
    - A cubic-millimeter 3D reconstruction of mouse V1, useful for visualizing V1 cell connectivity motifs as they are in-vivo. They furthermore offer tools to filter through cell types and discover marked synapses. The data is far from complete though.
- **Allen Brain Reference Atlases:** [https://atlas.brain-map.org/](https://atlas.brain-map.org/)
	- Contains atlases for mouse and human. 
- **Mouse Brain Connectivity Atlas:** [https://connectivity.brain-map.org/](https://connectivity.brain-map.org/)
    - This is a valuable resource in looking for cell projections from a specific region to another region.
	- E.g., see picture below:
	- ![Allen Brain Connectivity Atlas](/images/Allen Brain Connectivity Atlas.jpg)
- **Brain ISH**: Mouse: [https://mouse.brain-map.org/](https://mouse.brain-map.org/) \| Human: [https://human.brain-map.org/ish/search](https://human.brain-map.org/ish/search)
	- ISH (In-Situ Hybridization) shows the spatial location of gene expression in tissue
- **Cell Samples**: [https://celltypes.brain-map.org/data](https://celltypes.brain-map.org/data)
	- These contain previously-recorded cells along with morphological and electrophysiological properties, in both mouse and human
